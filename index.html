01 - Define the problem

The site was memorable, but the rental task was hard to follow.

Ling's Cars has a bold personality, but the experience makes users sort through competing colors, animations, promotions, and unclear calls to action before they can do the basic thing they came for: find a car and understand the deal.

Our redesign focused on first-time renters who need to compare cars quickly, see pricing clearly, and move into booking without feeling lost. The problem was not simply that the original website "needed a redesign." The deeper problem was that the interface competed with the user's decision-making.

Visual overload

Too many messages fought for attention at the same time.

Unclear hierarchy

Search, vehicle details, pricing, and booking were not prioritized clearly.

Low confidence

Users could not easily scan car options or confirm what they were paying for.

04 - Design decisions

Each screen was designed around the next decision the user needed to make.

Search first

The homepage starts with location because availability is the user's first real question.

Scannable car cards

Consistent images, names, prices, and buttons make comparison faster.

Focused details

The detail page separates specs and price breakdown from browsing.

Confirmation at checkout

The booking page repeats the selected car, dates, insurance, and total cost.

05 - Testing and outcomes

Testing checked whether people could complete the rental path without help.

What we tested

Five classmates were asked to find a car in Atlanta, compare two cars, view the total price, and begin booking.

What we heard

Users understood the new card layout, but some wanted stronger feedback after search and clearer checkout labels.

What changed

We added loading states, improved labels, made prices more visible, and kept navigation consistent across pages.

Finding Design response
Users hesitated after entering a location. Added loading feedback and clearer result context.
Users wanted price visibility before opening details. Kept daily rates visible on each car card.
Users looked for confirmation before checkout. Added a reservation summary with car, dates, insurance, and total.

06 - Final product

The final prototype creates a clearer path from browsing to booking.

Browse

Book

The finished DriveNow prototype includes a responsive homepage, results page, car details page, booking page, dark mode, loading states, and a clearer information hierarchy than the original experience.
